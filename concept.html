CONCEPT25


The cars user interface system will have a 57 inch touch screen display behind the steering wheel which will stretch from A-pillar to A-pillar and a vertically placed touch enabled control screen flowing down from the dash. All the functionalities of the car shall be controlled from these touch panels.

To provide distraction free seamless interaction while driving, controls are provided on the steering wheel. The steering wheel which pulls out only for non-autonomous modes, has two joystick controls sitting on ends of an OLED display screen. The hard buttons give essential feedback and enables the user to drive without taking their hands off the steering wheel.

The left joystick is used to navigate through the functionaities on the dash screen, which are grouped into a collection of cards. Whereas the actions of the right joystick changes depending on the menu card the user is currently on. The user can also customise its functions based on their need.

Adaptive Interface Design


All the functions of the car are neatly organized and displayed in the form of cards. The AI system learns the user's behaviour over a period of time and personalizes the dashboard layout to make it more easier and intuitive to use. The user can choose to have their smartphone application to be placed as a card on the dash for quick access. All their favourite social media apps and games will be readily available to them without having to take out the phone from their pocket.

The cards can be easily moved around the screen according to the users convenience by swiping on the touch screen or using the left joystick control on the steering wheel.

Drive Mode Functions


While driving the user uses the joysticks to control the interface. All the cards for drive related settings are placed on the drivers side of instrument cluster for quick access. It is also locked to this side of steering wheel to prevent accidental usage by any one in the passenger seat. The rest of the cards are placed in the center, within the reach of both passenger and driver.

Most of the times, the HUD which is located dead center of the users field of view displays only the navigation guides and feed from the obstacle detection system. But when any other card is moved over the instrument cluster card, some of its critical information is moved up on to HUD.

Driveless Mode


In Autonomous mode the steering wheel retracts into the dash creating more space around the user in the driver seat and touch becomes the only mode of interaction. The rear view displays are no longer visible and the display colors on the screen become more brighter to give an immersive experience.

Track Mode


Driving on the track is a highly dangerous activity and requires full attention on the part of the driver. The UI becomes very minimal for this reason, showcasing only driving related information on the screen.
